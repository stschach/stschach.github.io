    The pHyper [A]3

    The pHyper [A]3 was created for senior design. We met with a local water treatment facility and learned that they had to perform water sample titrations by hand to obtain the alkalinity, which is labor intensive. As a result, they used conductivity as a surrogate in order to determine system operation parameters. With alkalinity, however, they would be able to run more efficient cycles, and save a significant amount of time and money.
    The pHyper [A]3 is my senior design team's solution to that problem. Here is a brief demonstration of our Automatic Alkalinity Analyzer:


    We achieved alkalinity calculations to be within +/- 25% of the expected value from a hand titration with over 95% repeatability in about 2.5 minutes/cycle. Our device possessed a gravity-fed sample collection design, automated wash & drain functionality, wifi accessibility, a desktop GUI, and a sleek enclosure.

    Robotics

    The affectionately named In-Home Autonomous Companion pictured above was created in order to learn and understand the inner-workings of an embedded system. On a team of four, each of us started with a PCB, an MSP430, and a series of tasks completed throughout the semester, as documented in this report.


    Our team tackled GPS coordinate localization, instead of line-following, for the sake of the challenge. Fortunately, we were not disappointed. After several all-nighters and at least $50 worth of AAs, we finally achieved results worthy of posting to YouTube! This project was nothing but fun from the beginning.

    Mechatronics and Legos. This Mindstorms EV3 bot was built by a team of three as an investigation of PID tuning and control.

    Although the PID algorithm was the only new concept in this bot, we went through many hurdles to achieve neat path tracking, platooning, and collision avoidance with the EV3. We implemented several PID controllers as we discovered their usefulness.

    Wearable Biotech

    My team designed a wearable biosensor that detected abnormal muscle movement with EMG, and transmitted the data through bluetooth to the caretaker, so that a seizure would not go undetected.
    For the purposes of this project, we did not take the design to implementation.
